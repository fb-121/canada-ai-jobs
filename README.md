# AI Exposure of the Canadian Job Market

An interactive visualization of how susceptible major occupations in Canada are to AI and automation.

**Live site:** [fb-121.github.io/canada-ai-jobs](https://fb-121.github.io/canada-ai-jobs)

---

## What this is

This project takes every major occupation in Canada and asks: how much will AI reshape this role over the next 5-10 years? Each occupation gets an AI Exposure score from 0 to 10. The interactive treemap displays all occupations simultaneously -- area reflects employment size, colour reflects AI risk.

---

## Scope

This version covers **113 occupations mapped to specific 5-digit NOC 2021 unit group codes**. Employment and wage figures are sourced directly from Statistics Canada and ESDC -- no estimates. It does not cover all ~500 NOC unit groups; occupations were selected to represent the major sectors of the Canadian economy with sufficient employment data to be meaningful.

This was a deliberate decision. A smaller set of fully verified, sourced entries is more credible than a larger set with estimated figures. Accuracy over completeness.

A v2 expanding to the full ~500 NOC unit group dataset is planned.

## Data sources

| Data | Source | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Occupation structure | [NOC 2021 Version 1.0](https://www.statcan.gc.ca/en/subjects/standard/noc/2021/indexV1) | 5-digit unit group codes |
| Employment counts | [Statistics Canada 2021 Census](https://www12.statcan.gc.ca/census-recensement/2021/dp-pd/dv-vd/occ-pro/index-en.cfm) | Full-year, full-time workers who reported employment income in 2020. Part-time and self-employed not included. |
| Median wages | [ESDC LMI Wage Dataset 2025](https://open.canada.ca/data/en/dataset/adad580f-76b0-4502-bd05-20c125de9116) | Released November 2025. Hourly wages converted to annual at 2,080 hrs/year. |

Employment figures represent full-year, full-time workers who reported employment income in 2020, sourced directly from the 2021 Census. Part-time, seasonal and self-employed workers are not counted. Wages are from ESDC 2025 -- more current than the employment data, which is the most recent Census available. This vintage mismatch is documented in the site footer.

## Methodology

Each occupation is scored on a single **AI Exposure** axis (0-10) measuring how much AI will reshape the role -- combining direct automation (AI performing the work) and indirect displacement (AI making workers productive enough that fewer are needed).

**Scoring rubric:**

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 0-2 | Minimal -- physical work in variable environments |
| 3-4 | Low -- judgment and physical presence required |
| 5-6 | Moderate -- mixed digital and physical, AI-assisted |
| 7-8 | High -- digital-first, substantial task automation |
| 9-10 | Maximum -- core output fully AI-replicable today |

The exact scoring prompt used to generate AI exposure scores is published on the live site under "View scoring prompt" for full transparency and reproducibility.

**Important:** A high exposure score does not mean a job will disappear. It means AI will reshape how the work is done. Many high-exposure jobs will be reshaped, not replaced. The score does not account for demand elasticity, latent demand, regulatory barriers, or social preferences for human workers.

**Key signal:** Whether the job's core output is fundamentally digital. If a role can be performed entirely from a computer without physical presence, AI exposure is inherently high. Roles requiring manual dexterity, physical presence, real-time situational judgment, or trusted interpersonal relationships have a natural structural barrier.

Scores were generated by prompting Claude (Anthropic) with each occupation's NOC title and typical duties, then reviewed and adjusted for cross-occupation consistency. These are informed estimates, not a deterministic formula. Reasonable analysts could score some occupations differently.

## Limitations

- **National aggregates only.** Regional variation is not captured. Alberta's resource economy and Ontario's finance and tech sectors have meaningfully different AI exposure profiles that a national average flattens.
- **Unit group level, selected occupations.** Sub-occupations within each category may vary significantly in exposure.
- **2020 employment reference year.** The 2021 Census captured employment conditions in 2020, a COVID-affected year. Occupations in healthcare, hospitality and food services may show atypical counts. This is the most recent comprehensive occupational employment data available in Canada.
- **Not affiliated with Statistics Canada or ESDC.** This is an independent analysis.

## How to use

Single HTML file -- no build process, no dependencies to install. Clone the repo and open `index.html` in any browser, or deploy directly to GitHub Pages.

```bash
git clone https://github.com/fb-121/canada-ai-jobs
cd canada-ai-jobs
open index.html
```
